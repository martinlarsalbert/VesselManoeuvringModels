Manoeuvring parameter identification¶

Can the method that was introduced in the Ball drop example be applied to a more complex system? This will be attempted in this chapter based on the results from the manoeuvring simulation from the previous chapter.

Decoupling¶

The hydrodynamic derivatives in a mathematical model for ship manoeuvring should be identified by expressing the ODE as a OLS regression. There is a coupling between the sway and yaw equation in this model. These equations need to be decoupled (Wang et al., 2021) in a similar way as how it was done in the previous chapter. The simulation model can be expressed as:

\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}- X_{\dot{u}} + m & 0 & 0\\0 & - Y_{\dot{v}} + m & - Y_{\dot{r}} + m x_{G}\\0 & - N_{\dot{v}} + m x_{G} & I_{z} - N_{\dot{r}}\end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix}\dot{u}\\\dot{v}\\\dot{r}\end{matrix}\right] = \left[\begin{matrix}- m \left(- x_{G} r^{2} - r v\right) + \operatorname{X_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\\- m r u + \operatorname{Y_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\\- m x_{G} r u + \operatorname{N_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

\(X_{qs}\), \(Y_{qs}\), \(N_{qs}\) are the quasi static hydrodynamic force models, that contains the hydrodynamic derivatives (\(Y_{uv}\) etc.) that should be identified.

The PIT should be expressed as three regressions based on \(\dot{u}\), \(\dot{v}\) and \(\dot{r}\):

\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}\dot{u}\\\dot{v}\\\dot{r}\end{matrix}\right] = \left[\begin{matrix}A_{coeff} X_{X}\\B_{coeff} X_{Y}\\C_{coeff} X_{N}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Where \(A_{coeff}\), \(B_{coeff}\) and \(C_{coeff}\) are coefficient vectors from where the hydrodynamic derivatives can be obtained with decoupling as follows, where eq.eq_coeff has been inserted into eq.eq_simulator:

\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}- X_{\dot{u}} + m & 0 & 0\\0 & - Y_{\dot{v}} + m & - Y_{\dot{r}} + m x_{G}\\0 & - N_{\dot{v}} + m x_{G} & I_{z} - N_{\dot{r}}\end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix}A_{coeff} X_{X}\\B_{coeff} X_{Y}\\C_{coeff} X_{N}\end{matrix}\right] = \left[\begin{matrix}- m \left(- x_{G} r^{2} - r v\right) + \operatorname{X_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\\- m r u + \operatorname{Y_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\\- m x_{G} r u + \operatorname{N_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

And the right hand side of this equation can also be expressed with vectors:

\[\displaystyle X_{X} X_{qs} = - m \left(- x_{G} r^{2} - r v\right) + \operatorname{X_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\]
\[\displaystyle X_{Y} X_{qs} = - m r u + \operatorname{Y_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\]
\[\displaystyle N_{qs} X_{N} = - m x_{G} r u + \operatorname{N_{qs}}{\left(u,v,r,\delta \right)}\]

So that the parameter vectors \(X_{qs}\), \(Y_{qs}\), \(N_{qs}\) can be calculated from \(A_{coeff}\), \(B_{coeff}\) and \(C_{coeff}\) in the following way.

\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}- X_{\dot{u}} + m & 0 & 0\\0 & - Y_{\dot{v}} + m & - Y_{\dot{r}} + m x_{G}\\0 & - N_{\dot{v}} + m x_{G} & I_{z} - N_{\dot{r}}\end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix}A_{coeff}\\B_{coeff}\\C_{coeff}\end{matrix}\right] = \left[\begin{matrix}X_{qs}\\Y_{qs}\\N_{qs}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Summary¶

It seams that the hydrodynamic coefficients in a nonlinear manoeuvring simulation model can be identified by regressing the reversed simulation model. The example in this chapter used exact positions, velocities and accelerations, without any measurment error. This is ofcourse not possible when using real data from model test or full scale ship operations.
